Comparative Impact Analysis
True / False
When doing impact calculus, you should compare your impact against the opposing side's impact using dimensions like scope (how many people), magnitude (how severe), and probability (how likely) — not just assert that your impact is important in isolation.

Question 12 of 15
On-Case vs. Off-Case Refutation
True / False
On-case refutation attacks the internal logic of your opponent's argument (their warrant, evidence, or link chain), while off-case refutation introduces an entirely new argument on a different aspect of the bill that the opponent didn't address.
